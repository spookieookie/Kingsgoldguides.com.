## Farm Method

Use a high-level character to quickly clear old Cataclysm raid trash and encounters. Modern characters have enough damage to burn through content that was originally designed for raid groups, but you still need to respect the layout, boss triggers, and raid lockout behavior.

## Auction House Strategy

This farm should be managed like a hybrid raw gold and transmog route. Do not blindly vendor every item, but also do not assume every item is valuable. The correct workflow is to sort loot after the run, vendor obvious junk, auction stackable materials when the price is reasonable, and separately price-check every BoE or unusual-looking item.

### Listing Recommendations

- **Sell Embersilk Cloth and Cataclysm materials** when your realm price is healthy
- **Vendor gray items and low-value soulbound gear** quickly to convert the run into raw gold
- **Check BoE weapons, armor, and recipes** before vendoring anything
- **List rare-looking transmog** for 24 or 48 hours and be prepared to repost
- **Do not undercut expensive transmog aggressively** — slow-sale items need patience
- **Track several runs** before making claims about average gold per hour; one run is not enough data

## FAQ

**Is this a guaranteed high gold-per-hour farm?**

No. This is an old-raid farm. It can produce raw gold, materials, and sellable items, but the best auction-house value is RNG-dependent and server-dependent. Do not expect the same gold per hour across different realms or weeks.

**Where is the farm located?**

The main route shown in the video is inside Blackwing Descent in Burning Steppes, Eastern Kingdoms. This is accessible to high-level characters who can solo Cataclysm content.

**What should I loot?**

Loot everything. Vendor obvious junk, sell stackable materials when the market is healthy, and price-check BoE gear before vendoring it. Always carry an auction addon or use a pricing tool to avoid leaving gold on the table.

**Can I farm this endlessly?**

No. Because Blackwing Descent is a raid, lockout rules apply. You cannot infinitely reset the raid on a single character within the lockout window. You can run it again after the lockout resets, or use multiple characters to extend your weekly runs.

**What makes this farm useful?**

It is simple old-content farming with a mix of raw value and auction-house upside. The footage shows a straightforward clear that high-level characters can run with minimal mechanical difficulty. It is especially useful if you enjoy building a portfolio of transmog and old-raid materials.

**What is the difference between raw gold and transmog upside?**

Raw gold comes from direct vendor sales, boss loot, and coin drops. Transmog upside is the potential value of selling unique old-raid appearances on the auction house. The raw gold is reliable but modest; the transmog upside is less predictable but can be more profitable if you find the right items and list them on an active realm.

**How long does each run take?**

Depending on your character's damage, gear, and movement speed, a clear typically takes 8–12 minutes. The exact time varies based on how quickly you can burn through trash packs and boss encounters.

**Should I raid with a group or solo?**

This farm is designed for solo play using a high-level character. Soloing allows you to keep all the loot and control the pacing of the clear. Raiding in a group would split loot and reduce your personal income.

**What realm should I farm this on?**

Any realm works, but realm pricing for materials and transmog varies significantly. Test the market on your home realm before committing many runs. High-population realms often have more transmog buyers, but also more competition.
