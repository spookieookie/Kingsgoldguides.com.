## About This Farm

This Southern Barrens gold farm targets the infinite-respawn mob packs at **Bael Modan Excavation** in the Southern Barrens. The farm is designed for players who want old-world transmog inventory, cloth, and a chance at the **Pattern: Rich Purple Silk Shirt**—a rare old-world recipe that can command a large price on some realms.

The video shows the player farming the excavation-site humanoids around the dig pit, tents, ramps, ruins, and wooden walls. The goal is to kill fast, loot everything, and generate Auction House inventory from cloth, green gear, recipes, and rare transmog drops.

### How to Get There

Travel to the Southern Barrens in Kalimdor and head to Bael Modan Excavation. The video displays the Bael Modan Excavation location text while the player moves around the dig site. Once there, stay inside the compact excavation area rather than roaming across the entire zone.

### The Farming Loop

Run the farm as a **small circular loop**:

1. Start at the top or side of the excavation pit where the mob packs are visible
2. Tag the nearby Bael'dun / excavation enemies with instant abilities
3. Pull them into a tight cluster if your class can do it safely
4. Kill the group with AoE and immediately move to the next pack
5. Loop through the tents, ramps, ruins, and open center area
6. By the time the loop is complete, new mobs should be available again
7. Repeat the cycle

The farm is strongest when you maintain constant movement and minimize downtime. The area is small and the mobs keep coming back, so don't overcomplicate the route.

## How the Farm Works

The enemies around the excavation site **respawn quickly enough** that you can keep cycling the area. Start at one edge of the pit, clear the nearby mobs, move through the center, clear the packs near the tents and ruins, then circle back.

This matters because **old-world humanoids** can drop cloth, silver, green-quality gear, recipes, and transmog pieces. The value comes from **volume**. Every kill is another roll at saleable Auction House inventory, and the **rare pattern is the item that creates the large upside**.

## Auction House Strategy

**This is NOT a raw-gold-per-hour farm.** The correct model is **inventory creation**. The video title says "up to 200k" because a valuable rare pattern or transmog sale can create a large payout. Most sessions will be more modest until the expensive items drop and sell.

Treat every valuable drop as a **long-tail asset**:

- Use TradeSkillMaster, Auctionator, or another pricing addon before vendoring green items
- Post the Pattern: Rich Purple Silk Shirt as a premium recipe, not as junk loot
- **Avoid panic-undercutting.** Slow-sale items need patient reposting
- Use 24-hour or 48-hour listings depending on your auction deposit strategy
- Build a larger transmog inventory. One rare item may sit; many rare items create consistent chances at sales
- Track actual sales, not just tooltip market value. High listed value is not the same as liquid gold

## Who Should Run This Farm

Run this farm if you:

- Like old-world farms
- Have a fast AoE class
- Want to build a transmog or recipe inventory
- Enjoy the Auction House strategy game

**Skip it if** you need guaranteed immediate gold, because the high-value items depend on RNG and buyer demand.

## Reality Check

The phrase "make up to 200k" should be understood as **upside potential, not guaranteed result**. Profit depends on:

- Drop RNG (especially the rare pattern)
- Your realm's market demand and prices
- Sell-through rate for your transmog
- Your patience and reposting discipline

Most sessions will generate steady cloth and green transmog sales. The big 200k payout comes from lucky rare drops and finding the right buyer who values old-world transmog.

## Common Mistakes

- Calling this guaranteed 200k raw gold (it's not)
- Vendoring green items without checking transmog value
- Leaving the area before the respawn cycle kicks in
- Standing in one exact spot instead of looping the full excavation site
- Farming with full bags and losing time to cleanup
- Listing rare items too cheaply because they didn't sell immediately
