## Why This Farm Works

This route works because Tiragarde Sound has **clustered BFA ore and herb nodes** across compact terrain. Instead of waiting on a rare drop, the value comes from **consistent volume**:

- Every **Monelite Ore** and **Storm Silver Ore** becomes Auction House inventory
- BFA herbs naturally appear along the same path
- The route is flexible—if an ore node is missing, keep moving
- Profit comes from **motion and node uptime**, not fighting every mob in the area

The one-million-gold angle should be treated as a **cumulative market strategy**. You farm materials, stack them, list them on the Auction House, and repeat the loop. Do not expect a guaranteed one-million payout from one session.

## Required Setup

To farm this route effectively, you need:

- A character with access to **Battle for Azeroth** Kul Tiras content
- **Mining** trained for BFA nodes
- **Herbalism** strongly recommended (the route passes multiple herb nodes naturally)
- A **fast mount** (flying improves efficiency, but ground movement works)
- **Empty bags** (gathering routes lose value when you stop early to vendor or mail)
- **Auction pricing addon** such as TradeSkillMaster or Auctionator
- Optional: **GatherMate2** to track node history, **Routes** to map node clusters

## Step-by-Step Route

### Starting Point
Begin in Tiragarde Sound on the southern side of Boralus, near the **Kennings Lodge** and **Bridgeport** area. The video highlights the route on the map before beginning.

### The Loop

1. **Kennings Lodge & Hills** - Ride the loop through the hills and road network. Prioritize ore nodes on rock faces, ledges, roadside slopes, and basin edges.

2. **Bridgeport Side** - Continue toward the Bridgeport side of the loop. Mine and gather herbs near bridges, shallow water, and hillside transitions.

3. **Winterdeep Basin** - Sweep through Winterdeep Basin and nearby paths. This is where the video shows several material pickups, including both BFA herbs and ore nodes.

4. **Old Drust Road** - Follow the terrain back through Old Drust Road and the lower hills. Mine Storm Silver and Monelite nodes as they appear, then reconnect to the starting loop.

5. **Repeat** - Do not wait around for individual nodes. The route works because you keep moving until spawns rotate back in.

## Execution Notes

**Movement discipline matters more than combat.** Stay on the loop, check ledges and slopes for ore spawns, and only fight when mobs prevent gathering. The player in the video keeps the pace moving and gathers both ore and herbs—the correct operating model for this route.

Because BFA materials are commodity items, your actual result depends on:
- **Realm pricing** and regional supply
- **Player demand** (crafting, leveling, flipping)
- **Market conditions** (flooding or scarcity)

The safer claim is not "you will make one million gold today." The safer claim is **"this route can contribute to a million-gold inventory pipeline if repeated and listed correctly."**

## Auction House Strategy

- **Post buyer-friendly stack sizes** - Full stacks work for crafters; smaller stacks move when buyers need only a few materials
- **Check market value first** - Do not dump everything at the lowest price if the market is thin
- **Separate fast and slow movers** - Ore may move faster than transmog, but BFA demand can still fluctuate
- **Consider holding materials** - Old-expansion materials often spike when players level professions or finish achievements
- **Track value per loop** - If the route underperforms on your realm, switch to another farm instead of forcing bad ROI

## Best Class and Build

Any class can run the route, but the best performers have easy disengage tools and movement utility:

- **Druid** (ideal - best gathering convenience and movement)
- **Demon Hunter** (strong mobility and quick escapes)
- **Hunter** (distance and pet utility)
- **Monk** (speed and flexibility)

The more time you spend mounted and moving, the better the route performs.

## Common Mistakes

- **Standing still waiting for a single node respawn** - Keep moving instead
- **Skipping herbs even though the route passes them** - Gather everything for maximum value
- **Over-fighting mobs instead of gathering and moving** - Combat is secondary
- **Listing all materials at fire-sale prices** - Check market value first
- **Expecting one run to create one million gold** - This is a repeatable pipeline, not a jackpot farm

## Reality Check

Old-expansion gathering markets change constantly. Before farming for long sessions:

1. **Run one test loop**
2. **Price the materials** on your realm
3. **Compare expected gold per hour** against other farms
4. **Adjust your profit target** if your realm has weak BFA material demand

This guide still works as route documentation, but your actual profit depends on your realm's economic conditions.

## Who Should Run It

**Best for:**
- Players who want predictable material generation
