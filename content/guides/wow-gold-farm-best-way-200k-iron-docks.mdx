The farm demonstrates a full dungeon clear with a focus on volume and trash pack efficiency rather than a single rare spawn or fixed hyperspawn location.

## Iron Docks Route

The demonstrated route starts inside Iron Docks and moves through the early yard, platform, bridge, and dock combat areas. The video shows the player pulling trash packs, killing dense groups, moving toward boss rooms, and continuing until the later dungeon sections.

### Route Steps

1. **Enter Iron Docks** from the Iron Approach portal area in Gorgrond
2. **Clear the first open yard** and dock workers near the entrance
3. **Pull nearby trash groups** together where safe, then burn them down with AoE
4. **Move through the cannon and platform sections** instead of wasting time on empty side areas
5. **Continue across the dock pathways** and clear the larger Iron Horde packs
6. **Kill bosses and elite packs** as you naturally reach them during the route
7. **Loot every corpse**, including trash mobs and bosses, because the gold comes from total run volume
8. **After the run**, vendor trash, evaluate materials, and reset/repeat if you are farming in sessions

### Efficiency Rules

- Do not overthink individual mobs. Pull groups and clear fast
- Loot everything because small-value items add up over repeated runs
- Avoid long detours unless they add meaningful mob density
- Kill bosses when they are on the route, especially when the clear naturally leads into them
- Reset quickly after a completed route if your plan is to farm repeated dungeon sessions
- Track your actual results by run count, not by one lucky or unlucky pull

### What to Keep vs Vendor

- **Keep and price-check**: Sumptuous Fur and any material stacks with auction-house demand
- **Price-check before vendoring**: Bind-on-equip green, blue, or epic items
- **Always vendor**: Obvious gray-quality trash after each session
- **Check before deleting**: Any unusual transmog weapon or armor appearances
- **Track averages**: Do not use one run to judge the farm. Track multiple clears for a realistic average

## Reality Check: The 200,000 Gold Claim

The original 200,000 gold figure is **historical and should be framed as potential value, not guaranteed current gold per hour**. The video was recorded in 2020, and World of Warcraft economies shift with each expansion and realm. Current results depend on:

- Your realm's current auction-house prices for Sumptuous Fur and materials
- The speed at which you can clear Iron Docks on your character
- Whether materials and BoE items actually sell at posted prices
- Market saturation and player demand for Warlords-era transmog
- Expansion economy state (older content becomes less valuable as new content releases)

Modern players running this farm should track their own per-run averages instead of expecting the original video's 2020 numbers to replicate exactly.
